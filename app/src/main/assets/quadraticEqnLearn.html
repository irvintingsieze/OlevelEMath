1. Solve problems with inequalities

Q1. Solve an equation $4x^2+4x+1$=0 using the completing the square method. Step 1: Bring the constant to the RHS (Right Hand Side) by subtracting 1 to both sides.

$4x^2+4x=-1$

Step 2: Make the coefficient of $X^2$ one by dividing the entire equation by 4.
$x^2+x=-1/4$

Step 3: Add to both side $(1/2)^2$ $(\text"coefficient of x" /2)^2$ required to form a square on the LHS
$x^2+x+(1/2)^2=-1/4+(1/2)^2$

Step 4: Step 4: Form the square and simplify RHS (note: The RHS may not always be 0)
$(x+1/2)^2=0$

Step 5: Square root both sides
$x+1/2=0$

Step 6: Isolate the X on the LHS
$x=-1/2$

2. Using the formula

For $ax^2+bx+c=0$ where a,b and c are real numbers and a≠0

$$x={-b±√{b^2-4ac}}/{2a}$$

Q2. Solve $2x^2+3x+1$
$$x={-b±√{b^2-4ac}}/{2a}$$
$$={-3±√{3^2-4(2)(1)}}/{2(2)}$$
$$={-3±√{9-8}}/{4}$$
$$={-3±1}/{4}$$
$$=-1/2, -1$$
