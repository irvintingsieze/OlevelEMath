1. Laws of Indices

a) $a^x$ x $a^y=a^(x+y)$
b) $a^x÷a^y=a^(x-y)$ , if a≠0
c) $(a^x)^y = a^(xy)$
d) $a^x$ x $b^x=(ab)^x$
e) $a^x÷b^x=(a/b)^x$ , if b≠0
The above assumes that a and b are real numbers and x and y are positive integers.

2. Zero and Negative Indices

Assuming a is a real number and is not 0, a) $a^0=1$ (If a=0, $0^0$=0)

b) $a^(-x)=1/a^x$ (If a=0. ${1}/{0^x}$ = ∞)

3. Rational Indices

Assuming a, x and y are positive integers, a) $a^(1/x)= √^x{a}$
b) $a^(x/y)=(√^y{a})^x$

4. Compound Interest

The compound Interest formula is

$A = P (1 + R/100)^n$


where A is the total amount, P is the principal amount(the initial amount), R is the interest rate per year in %, and n is the number of years

5. Writing numbers in standard forms

A number is in standard form when expressed in the form of a x $10^n$, where n is an integer and 1≤a<10.

For example, 10900 in standard form would be $1.09x10^5$
