1. Circle Theorems

1. Perpendicular chord bisection

If a line OB extends from the centre to a chord AC and forms a right angle, it forms a perpendicular bisector of chord AC.
AB = BC

2.Tangent perpendicular to radius

If a radius (OB) of the circle meets the tangent (AC) of a circle, it forms a right angle OBC / OBA.

3. Tangents

If tangents of a circle extends to meet at a common point B, The tangents AB and BC are equal in length.
Secondly, the line that extends from centre of circle to meeting point B bisects angle ABC.
∠ ABO = ∠ OBC

4. Chords of same length

Chords which are equally distant from the centre of the circle have the same length.

5. Right angle in a semicircle

When 1.the triangle is in the circle, 2. all three ends of triangle touches the circumference and 3. the hypotenuse forms the diameter of the circle,
The angle opposite the hypotenuse is a right angle (∠ABC).

6. Angles in the same segment

Angles (∠ABC and ∠ADC) which are formed from the two same points (A and C) in the circle are equal. 7. Angle at centre equals to 2 angle at circumference

An angle formed at the center (∠AOC) is 2 times that of the angle in the circumference(∠ABC), given that both angles are formed from the same two points (A and C) in the circumference.
8.Angles of opposite segments

When all 4 points of the quadrilateral touches the circumference of the circle, opposite angles add up to 180°.
∠ACD=∠ABD
