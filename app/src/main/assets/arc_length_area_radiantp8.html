In this topic, we are going to learn how to
1. Identify the parts of a circle.
2. Convert between radian and degrees
3. Calculate length and area of a sector.

1. Identify the parts of a circle


Arc
An arc is a part of a curve/ circle. From the image above, BC is an arc.
Sector
A sector is a part of a circle enclosed by two radius and the included arc.
Chord
A chord is a straight line in the circle that extends to 2 points of the circle.
Segment
A segment is a part of a circle enclosed by a chord and the included arc.

Note: There is no need to memorise the definition. However, you MUST know how to identify the different parts of a circle.

2. Convert between radian and degrees


The above image will be used as a reference to describe the 3 learning objectives.
A radian of a circle is the RATIO of the arc length to the radius.

$∠BAC$= $s/r$ radians

1 radian = ${180°}/π$
1° = $π/{180°}$

3. Calculate length and area of a sector

With reference from the image above,

Using degrees,
Arc length of BC = ${∠BAC}/360° x 2πr $
Area of sector ABC = ${∠BAC}/360° x πr^2 $
Using radian,
Arc length of BC = r$θ$
Area of sector ABC = $1/2r^2θ$
Where θ is the ∠BAC in radians
