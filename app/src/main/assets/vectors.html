1. Representing Vectors

A vector shows the magnitude (distance) and direction between 2 points.


To represent the vector from point X to Y is ${XY}↖{→}$. The vector ${XY}↖{→}$ can also be defined as a variable eg. a with a curly line beneath. ${a}↙{~}$

As vectors involve direction, the vector $-{XY}↖{→}$ is conversely $-{a}↙{~}$.
As vectors involve magnitude, a vector $k{XY}↖{→}$ can used to represent a line of varying length parallel to ${XY}↖{→}$.

Point X is also ${OX}↖{→}$, where the starting point is the origin, O. Similarly, Point Y is ${OY}↖{→}$

2. Magnitude of a vector

The magnitude of a vector can also be seen as the distance the vector covers.


A vector can be written as a column matrix with 2 rows (2D vectors).

An example will be ${XY}↖{→}$= ${a}↙{~}$= $(\table \3; \4)$.
The top row will represent the distance the vector covers horizontally.
The bottom row will represent the distance the vector covers vertically

To calculate the magnitude of ${XY}↖{→}$ or ${a}↙{~}$ is by pythagoras theorem

Take the sum of the squares in the matrix and root the sum
${XY}↖{→}$ = $√{3^2 + 4^2}$ = 5.

If vectors cancels each other eg ${a}↙{~}$ - ${a}↙{~}$ , the product will be a zero vector ${0}↙{~}$
${a}↙{~}$ - ${a}↙{~}$ = ${0}↙{~}$

3. Addition and Subtraction of Vectors

To add 2 vectors, the 2 letters closest to the " + " sign must be the same.
The resulting vector will consist of the two outer letters joined together.

Eg. ${XY}↖{→}$ + ${YZ}↖{→}$ = ${XZ}↖{→}$



In the image above, we also can tell that if ${XY}↖{→}$ = ${a}↙{~}$, and ${YZ}↖{→}$ = ${b}↙{~}$, ${XZ}↖{→}$ = ${a}↙{~}$+ ${b}↙{~}$



As vectors is not position specific, we can also say that ${XY}↖{→}$ = ${a}↙{~}$+ ${b}↙{~}$

For subtraction, the first letter of both vectors must be the same.
The 2nd letter of the the second vector and the 2nd letter of the first vector will be the resulting vector.
Eg. ${YX}↖{→}$ - ${YZ}↖{→}$ = ${ZX}↖{→}$



As point X is ${OX}↖{→}$ and point Y is ${OY}↖{→}$, we can tell that ${XY}↖{→}$ is also ${OY}↖{→}$ - ${OX}↖{→}$
